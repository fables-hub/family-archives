Jorune Bloodline

House Ashvale Pureblood · Branch · Est. 13th Century Prominent

Geographic & Historic Origin

  • Ancestral land: Wales
  • Current territory: Wales
  • Founder: Geraint o dŷ Jorune
  • Current head: Norrix Ashvale

The Ashvale family is a notable pureblooded family in Wales... but perhaps not known for entirely positive reasons. "Eccentric", some neighbors would call them. "Mad", others would challenge. Many speculate the pure blood of their lineage has led to magical curses that affected the minds... Though, it wouldn't explain how those who enter the family outside of the blood bond are content within the Ashvale manor. Some have even posed that the family are all vampires, though there's never been any evidence to support this accusation. Regardless of what afflicts the family, many can agree the Ashvales are simply not normal.

Identity & Culture

  • Overarching value: Autonomy

Core Values

  • Core Value #1 - Individuality Without Shame: Eccentricity and unusual interests are welcomed by the Ashvales. "Strange" is not a flaw, but rather an act of bravery.
  • Core Value #2 - Loyalty to Kin: Ashvales are fiercely loyal to their family, whether by blood, marriage, or their own choosing.
  • Core Value #3 - Indulgence of the Obscure: Ashvales value that which is often overlooked by society, whether it's old magic, outdated traditions, or "odd" wizardry. Curiosity is cultivated and housed safely within their walls.

Taboos

  • Taboo #1 - Public Conformity for Approval: An Ashvale who dares hide their true nature to gain social favor or popularity is considered a traitor to their family.
  • Taboo #2 - Weaponizing Family Secrets: The Ashvale secrets are numerous and macabre; using them for leverage or blackmail is a grave violation.
  • Taboo #3 - Harm Without Purpose: Cruelty for the sake of cruelty is forbidden among Ashvales; they may be morbid and macabre, but they are not evil. Any harm must have clear intent, justification, and necessity.

Specialty Disciplines

  • Potioneering
  • Herbology
  • Anatomy & medical wizardry

Inter-Family Relations

Allies

Neutrals

  • Greensong: Distant relatives from the Jorune bloodline.
  • Moss: Distant relatives from the Jorune bloodline.

Enemies

  • Jorune: Progenitor family; conflicting views on the macabre and morbid.
House Greensong Halfblood · Branch · Est. 15th Century Obscure

Geographic & Historic Origin

  • Ancestral land: Wales
  • Current territory: England
  • Founder: Isambard ap Jorune (Angelized to Greensong)
  • Current head: Rowland Greensong

The Greensong lineage began with a fracture within the Jorune family; while the main branch wished to emphasize old magic and stick to the traditions and duties of their family line, the Greensong branch desired to retire their loyalties to protecting the secrets of Merlin and Arthurian legend and instead expand their knowledge via cultural influence and trade, to know what other witches and wizards knew. The Greensong branch diverted on neutral terms with the Jorune tree, but still remains loyal to archiving their mother family's findings with the rest of their knowledge.

Identity & Culture

  • Overarching value: Knowledge

Core Values

  • Core Value #1 - Knowledge is Sacred: Knowledge is meant to be preserved and passed onto each generation. The failure or refusal to take knowledge handed down to one's generation-- the failure to learn-- is considered a moral failing.
  • Core Value #2 - Precision Over Passion: Emotional outbursts and impulsivity are frowned upon; Greensongs conduct themselves via analysis, restraint, and research. Emotional reactions are considered a lapse in judgement.
  • Core Value #3 - Lifelong Pursuit: Education does not end with schooling; it is a Greensong's duty to continue to learn whenever possible. They should seek to master their skills and career paths, and should ultimately contribute at least one small offering to the fount of knowledge to be passed to the next generation.

Taboos

  • Taboo #1 - Willful Ignorance: The refusal to learn or change one's opinion when given evidence that contradicts their position is considered willful ignorance. Not knowing something is forgiveable, but choosing not to know, or refusing to acknowledge evidence to keep an opinion, is not.
  • Taboo #2 - Falsifying Knowledge: Knowledge is always to be written by an objective hand when meant to be disseminated to the public; falsifying records or rewriting documented history to favor personal bias is an unforgivable taboo.
  • Taboo #3 - Withholding Knowledge: While secrecy may be necessary in certain career paths, withholding knowledge-- whether for leverage, spite, or dominance-- is unacceptable. Knowledge may be guarded, but never for personal ego.

Specialty Disciplines

  • History of Magic
  • Magical Law
  • Cartography and charting

Inter-Family Relations

Allies

  • Jorune: The Jorune family has since made peace with the Greensongs after their departure, and the two work closely together for the sake of knowledge.

Neutrals

  • Ashvales: Distant relatives; they still share knowledge with one another but find each others' core values to be "bizarre".
  • Mosses: Distant relatives; they aren't enemies, but they don't hold each other in the highest of regards due to differing values.
