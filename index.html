House Ashvale Pureblood · Branch of House Jorune · Est. 14th Century Prominent

Geographic & Historic Origin

  • Ancestral land: Wales
  • Current territory: Wales
  • Founder: Geraint o dŷ Jorune
  • Current head: Norrix Ashvale

The Ashvale family is a notable pureblooded family in Wales... but perhaps not known for entirely positive reasons. "Eccentric", some neighbors would call them. "Mad", others would challenge. Many speculate the pure blood of their lineage has led to magical curses that affected the minds... Though, it wouldn't explain how those who enter the family outside of the blood bond are content within the Ashvale manor. Some have even posed that the family are all vampires, though there's never been any evidence to support this accusation. Regardless of what afflicts the family, many can agree the Ashvales are simply not normal.

Identity & Culture

  • Overarching value: Autonomy

Core Values

  • Core Value #1 - Individuality Without Shame: Eccentricity and unusual interests are welcomed by the Ashvales. "Strange" is not a flaw, but rather an act of bravery.
  • Core Value #2 - Loyalty to Kin: Ashvales are fiercely loyal to their family, whether by blood, marriage, or their own choosing.
  • Core Value #3 - Indulgence of the Obscure: Ashvales value that which is often overlooked by society, whether it's old magic, outdated traditions, or "odd" wizardry. Curiosity is cultivated and housed safely within their walls.

Taboos

  • Taboo #1 - Public Conformity for Approval: An Ashvale who dares hide their true nature to gain social favor or popularity is considered a traitor to their family.
  • Taboo #2 - Weaponizing Family Secrets: The Ashvale secrets are numerous and macabre; using them for leverage or blackmail is a grave violation.
  • Taboo #3 - Harm Without Purpose: Cruelty for the sake of cruelty is forbidden among Ashvales; they may be morbid and macabre, but they are not evil. Any harm must have clear intent, justification, and necessity.

Specialty Disciplines

  • Herbology
  • Anatomy & medical wizardry

House Forte Pureblood · Est. 14th Century Prominent

History

The Forte family has been a long-standing bloodline in the heart of Italy; in the modern era, they are viewed as enforcers of order and peace for their wizarding community, but their history lengthy and drenched in blood. There was a time when the small wizarding community shared the same village as muggles, back in the 1300's. After an outbreak of witch scares that resulted in witches and wizards being murdered, the remaining magical lines decided to separate themselves from the nessuna magia— muggles, the English called them.

During the waning of the Renaissance and the swell of the Inquisition, more witches and wizards began to disappear. Several prominent families were called forward, the famiglia Forte being one of them, to protect their wizarding village from invading muggles who seemed to be getting past their defenses. As a result, The L'epurazione Della non Magia took place, and as a result, there was a slaughter of muggles within a fifty-mile radius of their village, largely headed by the famiglia Forte. It was explained in whispers by the muggles that it was a second wave of the black plague... but of course, the wizarding world knew better.

In the modern era, the famiglia Forte's hands have been significantly less bloody— but those in their community remember their valiant fight to preserve their kind, and the family is held in high regard as a result. Many members have prominent positions within the Italian Ministry of Magic and as aurors. Some, however, are simply enforcers of justice however they see fit— or wherever the money takes them.

House Heraux Pureblood · Est. 12th Century Prominent

History

The Heraux family; they were once among the epitome of greatness. Once sharing a strong bloodline with the Juneau family, centuries ago, the tree was split and the Heraux branch broken off when the ancestor of the Heraux's bloodline, Dionysus, was found copulating and reproducing with muggles. Considered a scandalous disgrace, the Heraux bloodline was cut off from all fortune and connection. They were left destitute, and to the purebloods of the world, they were right where they belonged in the muck and mud.

But the Heraux bloodline would not be so easily snuffed out. In spite of living in abject and inherited poverty over the generations, they not only survived, but they thrived. Until recent years, the mere existence of the Heraux line was a black eye to purebloods, and as late as the second Wizarding War, members of their family were targeted and murdered in an attempt to finally choke out the weed they'd become.

Purebloods believed they were slaying a hydra when they tried to cut down the Heraux family— but true to the nature of the hydra, for every head they cut out, three have grown back. The Heraux family, needless to say, is going absolutely nowhere.

    House Temnik Vampire · Est. 13th Century Obscure

    History

    The Temnik family is a vampire lineage, established around the 13th century near Lake Bled in Slovenia. Historically, the family has been characterized by their political heirarchy within their family: while the main family is handed down through birthright (as their species of vampires is able to have children), the full family consists of both bloodright and being turned. The current ruling heirarchy is always referred to as the "Prince", regardless of gender. They are the top of the family, and have final say in all family business. The second-most ruling heirarchy is the Sentinel, which is the Prince's personally chosen right hand. Beneath them are the Advisor and the Enforcer, the former of who represents both the Prince and Sentinel, and the socialites beneath them who voice their concerns, to bridge communication between the two. The latter ensures all decrees issued by the Prince are maintained, and if broken, the infractor is punished. The rest of the family have varying positions, but are referred to as "socialites"-- the ones who keep everything running smoothly and bring prosperity to the family.

    The Temnik family ages slowly, but is not immortal; as such, they allow new members into their ranks to ensure a strong line. Turning normal humans into vampires is not on a whim, and must be approved by the Prince in order to ensure safety and protection of their family. Not just anyone is allowed into their home, and it is considered a great honor and privilege to be approved by the Prince.
